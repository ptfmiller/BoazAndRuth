Our Own Hometown Hero

Boaz & Ruth founder, Martha Rollins, was recently named an Allen & Allen Hometown Hero. This is no shock to us - Martha has been a hero in Highland Park for many years. We're so thrilled she was honored in this way. The Hometown Heroes award honors individuals who are responsible for community initiatives and acts of kindness. She attended a special concert where the 50 heroes were featured. We're sure she was dancing the night away. Congratulations, Martha!

People Woven Together for Beauty and Power

The old brick arch in our Harvest Thrift Store stands as a symbol of the kind of structure we seek to achieve at Boaz & Ruth. We envision our organization as most effective when it is held up by everyone pushing together as the bricks in an arch—supporting and empowering the vision.

The body does not consist of one limb or organ but many. If the ear should say, “Because I am not the eye, I do not belong to the body,” would that make it any less a part? If the whole body were an eye how would we hear? If the whole body were an ear, how would we smell? The eye is not able to say to the hand, “I have no need of you”. So there is no room for division or discord between the parts of the body. The members all-alike have a mutual interest in and care for one another. (Paraphrase I Corinthians 12:13-26)

The above paraphrase represents the way this program is designed to function. Every position is important, and everyone’s job relates to all the other roles in this mission. Working as a team strongly united, we will do well and will meet the goals we have set.
